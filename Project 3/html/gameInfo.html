NBA Jam

NBA Jam was initially released in 1993 for the Sega Genesis console. It is the first game in the NBA Jam game series. It is most noted for the announcer saying, 'He's on fire!'.

    The in-game players are also able to defy physics with their ability to jump several times their own height and dunk a flaming basketball.

Sonic and Knuckles

Released on October 18, 1994, Sonic 3 and Knuckles was a well-received game with over 1 million copies sold.

    It was intended to be a single game, but was sold as two due to time constraint issues.

  Sonic 3 and Knuckles could actually be 'bridged' in a sense, since the player could use elements from both games using 'Lock-on' technology.

Golden Axe

This action-packed side-scrolling game was released in 1989. Golden Axe is set in a medieval world where the goal is to find a legendary weapon known as the Golden Axe.

    The player can use one of three warriors to battle their way to finding the legendary weapon. All three warriors have been greatly affected by the game's nemesis 'Death Adder'.

  Each warrior is also gifted with their own special kind of magic: the most powerful user would be Tyris, the amazoness.

Donkey Kong 64

Donkey Kong 64 is one of many games within the Donkey Kong series. Releasing in 1999 to the Nintendo 64, it is one of the best-selling games in the Nintendo 64 console history; selling 2.3 million copies in just 2 months.

    Throughout the game, the player will unlock 5 members of the Kong family while facing against the antagonist: King K. Rool.

Star Fox 64

Releasing to North America in 1997, Star Fox 64 is a 3-D scrolling aerial combat game based off of the original Star Wars.

    The player will play as Fox McCloud, who is the leader of 3 other pilots (Falco Lombardi, Peppy Hare, Slippy Toad) that battle along-side Fox.

    This game has alternate paths that the player can traverse to beat the game. The amazing work put into this game let Star Fox 64 to be noted as one of the best video games ever made!

Golden Eye 007

Golden Eye 007 or Golden Eye, was released in August of 1997. It was one of the best-selling games on Nintendo 64 with 8.09 million games sold.

    Golden Eye follows the story of the secret agent known as James Bond and his thrilling missions. It is a first person shooter with a story-mode and an arcade mode.

    Golden Eye included a stealth element to the game, to where the more stealthy the player, the easier it would be to complete the missions. Setting off alarms could spell disaster for the player making the mission near impossible to accomplish.

Street Fighter II

Street Fighter II: The World Warrior was released in 1992 for the Super Nintendo. The series is a well-known fighting game that began in 1987.

    This game features a combo system with several fighters to choose from, each having their own technique.

    While in battle, players can jump, crouch, move back and forth, and block to outsmart their enemies.

Mega Man X

Capcom's Mega Man X was released to the Super Nintendo in Japan in 1993 and to the US and Europe in 1994.

    MMX is about an android named Mega Man X, or X, that was created by a legendary scientist by the name of Dr. Light. X was created to have emotions and free will.

    X is found several years later by a scientist named Dr. Cain. Cain replicates X, but the replicated androids (reploids) become evil and must be hunted down and stopped. It is the players mission to stop their evil-doings and bring peace back to the world.

Super Castlevania IV

Super Castlevania IV is a side scroll platformer that was released in 1991. The player will play as Simon Belomnt as he journeys through 11 levels to defeat his foes.

    Starting off with 5 lives, the player must make it to the end without losing them all. Once all lives are lost, the game is over. Player health can be restored by eating food or consuming a Magic Crystal which is dropped after each boss is defeated.

    Simon is armed with a whip that primarily has 3 uses: attack, defense and traversing terrain. Simon can also use secondary weapons at the cost of health points.

Jet Grind Radio

Jet Grind Radio is a action/sport game where the player will control one of the members of the youth gang, The GGs, to skate through Tokyo, tagging spots with graffitti and evading the police.

    The player can do all sorts of stunts/tricks while traversing the streets of Shibuya and Shinjuku. They can even graffitti while doing tricks like grinding rails.

    JGR was Developed by Smilebit and published in June of 2000 by Sega. It was the first game to use the Cel-shaded art style due to the development team's desire to stray away from the anime/manga game visuals.

Sonic Adventure

Sonic Adventure, released in 1998, is a 3-D platformer that features other types of game elements such as role-playing and action. On their mission to defeat Dr. Robotnik, the player can play as 6 different characters like Sonic The Hedgehog, 'Tails' Prower and Knuckles The Echidna.

    The player begins in a hub world that links to each different level, which are called Action Stages. The character's objectives for each stage are different, so they must be played differently to be succesful in completing the stage.

    This game received critical acclaim and sold 2.5 million copies.

Rayman 2: The Great Escape

Released to a total of 10 consoles/platforms, Rayman 2: The Great Escape was critically acclaimed upon release for raising the standards for 3-D graphics, level design and gameplay.

    The player controls the main protagonist, Rayman, as he battles with an evil group of Robot pirates which have invaded his home world (Glade of Dreams). At the beginning of the game, the player will find themselves captured and being help prisoner by the invaders aboard their prison ship known as 'The Buccaneer'.

    Together with the help of Rayman's guide, Murfy, the player must traverse the world in order to find a way to defeat the evil invaders.
